Hi there! I'm Jennifer — a software engineer and founder working at the intersection of AI and healthcare. Welcome to my corner of the web.

## Now

I'm the founder of [.implement](https://dotimplement.ai), a software company building developer tools for AI and NLP applications in healthcare. Our flagship project is [HealthChain](https://github.com/dotimplement/HealthChain), an open-source Python SDK for deploying and testing healthcare AI, with native [FHIR](https://www.hl7.org/fhir/) support and EHR connectivity. It has a growing open-source community, has been featured in the [TLDR AI](https://tldr.tech/ai) newsletter, and I've presented it at the Google NHS AI Hackathon and the NHS Python Open Source Community conference.

Having worked at the unique intersection of research and software development in the NHS, I believe healthcare AI needs far more robust, transparent tooling for testing, evaluation, and orchestration — and that's what I'm building.

## Before

For three and a half years I was a senior software developer and honorary researcher between the [Institute of Health Informatics](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/health-informatics/ucl-institute-health-informatics) at [UCL](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/) and the [UCLH BRC Clinical Research Informatics Unit](https://www.uclhospitals.brc.nihr.ac.uk/clinical-research-informatics-unit). There I led the development of [MiADE](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/health-informatics/research/medical-information-ai-data-extractor-miade) (Medical information AI Data Extractor), a clinically evaluated NLP system for real-time diagnosis extraction at the point of care. MiADE is deployed in live clinical use at two major NHS hospitals — UCLH and Great Ormond Street (GOSH) — integrating with Epic via HL7. You can read about its design and implementation in our paper in *BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making* (see below).

I was also technical lead on a £605k EPSRC/UKRI-funded [research project](https://www.ukri.org/news/13-million-for-22-ai-for-health-research-projects/) using large language models to optimise diagnosis extraction, leading the LLM engineering and contributing to study design and multi-site model evaluation across UCLH and GOSH.

Earlier, I was one of the first two engineers to launch [Grow Football](https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/the-club/football-development/grow-football/), a computer-vision-powered coaching app with partnerships including Tottenham Hotspur, where I built ML architectures for object detection, pose estimation, and AR.

My route into software ran through neuroscience. I worked on the [neural basis of spatial navigation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ETaZAyK4IVM) in the [Barry lab](https://barry-lab.com/) at UCL, where my interest in software and machine learning first developed. This led me to one of the [most selective computer science conversion degrees](https://www.admissionreport.com/imperial-college-london/msc-computing-science), where I dabbled in deep learning and brain-computer interfaces — you can find my [thesis here](/assets/report.pdf). I hold an MSc in Computing Science from [Imperial College London](https://www.ic.ac.uk/), and an MSc in Clinical Neuroscience and a BSc in Human Sciences from [UCL](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/).
