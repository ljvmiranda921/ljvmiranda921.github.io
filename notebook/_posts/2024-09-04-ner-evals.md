<span class="firstcharacter">I</span>t has been a year since I released [calamanCy](https://github.com/ljvmiranda921/calamancy) and its accompanying BERT-based models.
They were pretty good at the time, even surpassing LLM performance ([Miranda, 2023](https://aclanthology.org/2023.sealp-1.2.pdf)).
However, newer versions of GPT were released, and recent LLMs even boast multilingual capabilities.
I guess it's high time to refresh my evals and post them here.

In this blog post, I will be evaluating on the [Tagalog TLUnified-NER](https://huggingface.co/datasets/ljvmiranda921/tlunified-ner) dataset (size: 782 sentences) and the Tagalog subsets of the Universal NER project ([Mayhew et al., 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.naacl-long.243/)) (size: 149 sentences). 
I evaluated on the following models:

- GPT-4 newer models
- GliNER
- Aya-23 8B and 101B
- Llama 3.1 8B and 70B
- SEA-LION and SeaLLM (tgl and ceb only)
